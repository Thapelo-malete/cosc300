Suicide Prevention and Awareness

If you or someone you know is struggling with suicidal thoughts, it's important to know that you are not alone. There are resources available to help you get through this difficult time.

Signs of Suicidal Thoughts

  • Expressions of hopelessness, helplessness, or worthlessness
  • Talking about feeling trapped, desperate, or like there's no way out
  • Increased use of drugs or alcohol
  • Withdrawal from friends, family, and social activities
  • Unusual mood swings, including anger, irritability, and sadness
  • Reckless behavior or taking unnecessary risks

How to Get Help

If you or someone you know is experiencing suicidal thoughts, it's important to seek help immediately. Here are some resources that can provide support:

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline:0800 567 567
  • Crisis Text Line: Text 0800 567 567 to connect with a crisis counselor
  • Local mental health services: Check with your doctor or insurance provider for a referral to a mental health professional in your area

Remember, there is always hope, and there is always help available.
